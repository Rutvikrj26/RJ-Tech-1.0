Computational Fluid Dynamics

Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) is a branch of fluid mechanics that uses numerical analysis and data structures to analyze and solve problems that involve fluid flows. Computers are used to perform the calculations required to simulate the free-stream flow of the fluid, and the interaction of the fluid (liquids and gases) with surfaces defined by boundary conditions.

First we choose the design alternatives. Computer Aided Design

Computer-aided design (CAD) is the use of computers (or workstations) to aid in the creation, modification, analysis, or optimization of a design. CAD software is used to increase the productivity of the designer, improve the quality of design, improve communications through documentation, and to create a database for manufacturing. CAD output is often in the form of electronic files for print, machining, or other manufacturing operations.

Wind Farm Design using ALM Technique

The world is currently moving towards Renewable Energy Sources. Wind Turbines are one of the leading Technologies in the field. In this project of ours, we have tried to use the Actuator Line Modelling Technique wherein we replace the turbine blades with single lines and manually apply the forces. One of the major challeneges forced was to capture the turbulence in the system. The Flow seperation also provides with several challenges in ALM Modelling Technique at the operational Reynolds number. The project is all about creating a workflow for low computational cost Wind Farm simulation.

Combined Design Package (Hardware and Software) for Mini Bio-Diesel Reactor

Our main goal in this project was to produce a design package(both software and hardware). A mathematical model with the variables time, amount of reactants and amount of catalyst was developed. The model was then coded using Matlab and Python(Open Sourced) to optimize the values of variables.Further, a gneneral Reactor design was taken and modified customly for our specific purpose. Then a Cost-Benefit analysis was performed to ease the Manufacturing and reduce the overall cost.

Thermal Analysis of Hemispherical Furnace

This project mainly focuses on the development of a better, more efficient, more ergonomic and cost-efficient Furnace. A mathematical Model was created based on the physical working of the Furnace and then the coded version of the model in Matlab was used to optimize the design parameters.
